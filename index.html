Ursula K. Le Guin

Hugo Award Winning Author of Utopian Fiction

“Ursula Le Guin’s writing has reconceived the literary aspirations, and redefined the expressive possibilities, of every genre she’s touched.” - Max Rudin

“Like all great writers of fiction, Ursula K. Le Guin creates imaginary worlds that restore us, hearts eased, to our own.” - The Boston Globe

“It’s impossible to overstate the quality and significance of her writing, which means so much to me personally not just as a writer but as a human being...” - Jo Walton

Ursula K. Le Guin was an American Novelist known for her speculative fiction writing, whose body of work includes more than 50 pieces, spanning volumes of poetry, fiction, and non-fiction novels, essays, translations, and more.

She won multiple awards for her work and, perhaps more importantly, has been cited as a major influence to current authors, such as Jo Walton and Ada Palmer, and creatives across mediums. Le Guin's Earthsea world was translated to animation in the Studio Ghibli film 'Tales From Earathsea (2006)'.

Outside of the reception of her work itself, Le Guin was considered an influential figure in wider social movements such as feminism, and explored themes of the effects of capitalism and potential utopian societies in her works.

For more than 50 years, Le Guin continued to produce new works and inspire future generations of writers and in 2003 she was named one of the Science Fiction and Fantasy Writers of America's Grand Masters.

Le Guin wrote more than 20 novels, and won multiple Nebula, and Hugo awards for her work. Some of her novels include:

  • The Books of Earthsea
  • The Word for World Is Forest, Putnam 1976, Berkley 1976.
  • The Dispossessed: An Ambiguous Utopia, Harper & Row 1974, Avon 1975.
  • The Left Hand of Darkness, Walker 1969, Ace 1969, Harper & Row 1980.
  • The Lathe of Heaven, Scribner’s 1971, Avon 1972, Scribner’s 2008.
  • Lavinia, Harcourt 2008.

Le Guin also wrote various works of non-fiction, including essays and translations, as well as poetry. Some of her poetic works include:

  • So Far So Good, Copper Canyon Press 2019.
  • Out Here, Raven Press 2010.
  • Late in the Day, PM Press 2015.
  • Finding My Elegy, Houghton Mifflin Harcourt 2012.
  • Incredible Good Fortune, Shambhala 2006.
